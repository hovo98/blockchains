Gravity DEX competition takeaways

The Gravity DEX incentivized testnet was an important preparatory piece for the mainnet. The on-boarding process, system testing, soft fork and data analytics will serve to make the product more fluid at launch. The competition saw over 11,000 active traders with more than 700,000 transactions happening over the course of the game. 

Congratulations to the 3,598 highly active traders and 11 bug bounty winners. They will be receiving their share of the $200,000 ATOM prize pool as well as their share of the sponsor token prizes valued at $120,000. If you were part of the winning group, an email was sent last week to inform you of the process. The prize distribution to the winners will be completed by May 31st. 

Thank you to all the players that made the Gravity DEX competition such a success. What were some of the player highlights?

  • 89% of all players executed at least one transaction

  • 36% of all players executing transactions, executed more than 50 during the game

  • 14% of traders had a swap amount of more than $100K with a median swap volume of $14.9K

Gravity unites a Cosmos DEX and bridge to Ethereum

While the Gravity DEX game is over, exciting developments are coming. Gravity is now the uniting force between a Cosmos DEX and a bridge between Ethereum and Cosmos. The combination of the most advanced interchain DEX and a secure decentralized bridge to Ethereum will enable the Cosmos ecosystem to pull billions of dollars of capital and liquidity toward it. 

Additionally, using Inter-Blockchain Communication (IBC) to enable swaps and pools of digital assets between blockchains in the Cosmos network and beyond, the utility of the Cosmos Hub and its native ATOM token is vastly increased.

New governance proposal passed: lower minimum deposit

A Cosmos governance proposal (prop#47) was put forward to reduce the current amount of the governance deposit fee. The deposit fee of 512 ATOM has stayed the same since Cosmos mainnet launch. The proposal, which was just approved by the ATOM holders on May 21st, is lowering the deposit fee to 64 ATOM in order to support an increase in governance activity for smaller stakeholders.

European Central Bank is introduced to Cosmos

A digital Euro is an ECB initiative to ‘guarantee that citizens in the Euro area can maintain costless access to a simple, universally accepted, safe and trusted means of payment.’ It would be an electronically based monetary form of the Euro and issued by the Eurosystem (the ECB and national central banks).

Recently, the Tendermint team was invited to present to the ECB the vision of Cosmos Cash, a regulatory compliant e-money token.
