Cosmos Hub Gravity DEX governance proposal is live

A few weeks ago, Mission Control highlighted the release of the Cosmos SDK Liquidity Module and a request for comment on the signal proposal to implement it into the Cosmos Hub as the Gravity DEX. After the  ATOM' s community feedback, the Gravity DEX formal governance proposal (prop#38) is now under active voting until March 17th.

The Cosmos Hub Gravity DEX is a key part of the Cosmos interchain vision for an open decentralized exchange between independent blockchains. The Gravity DEX lets users create a liquidity pool with a pair of tokens, provide liquidity by depositing reserve tokens into the liquidity pool, and trade tokens using the liquidity pool.

Cosmos Hub gets faster with the new Gaia release

Last week, a new version of Gaia was released. Gaia is the first implementation of the Cosmos Hub, built using the Cosmos SDK. The new Gaia release means that the new Stargate-enabled Cosmos Hub-4 is much faster at powering up. Increasing Cosmos Hub connectivity speeds increases the overall performance for stakeholders.

New community-led development to build a Smart Contract Hub

Cosmos community members have started an initiative to ‘take the load off of the Cosmos Hub by running a dedicated Smart Contract Hub called Juno.’ JUNO holders will consist of people who held their ATOMs in non-custodial wallets at the time of the Stargate Upgrade. The project is still in its initial phase, so now is the time to get involved and shape the future of Juno. 

The team is making a call-to-action to validators and developers who want to get involved. This will be one of the first community organized projects in the Cosmos ecosystem. The Reddit announcement gives a quick overview of the project’s objectives with new upcoming initiatives outlined on Twitter.

Osmosis - the AMM Laboratory in the Cosmos

The team behind Osmosis explained their ideas on the Cosmos Hub native DEX and how Osmosis fits into the DEX narrative. If you haven't been following, Osmosis aims to be an automated market maker (AMM) secured by the Cosmos Hub. What's interesting in their most recent publication are the three main features that they believe should exist on the Cosmos Hub and the synergistic relationship the Osmosis AMM and the Cosmos Hub will have over time. The three identified features for the Hub are:

  • shared security
  • staking derivatives
  • universal IBC adapter

The team summarizes 'Osmosis isn’t a module on the Cosmos Hub. But it will be a chain of the Cosmos Hub' further supporting the many chains, one ecosystem philosophy behind Cosmos.
